ARRI’s Small Update, Big Set Lesson

The news

ARRI released ALEXA 35 SUP 6.0.2 on May 4, 2026 for the ALEXA 35, ALEXA 35 Xtreme, and ALEXA 35 Live.

This is not a flashy camera launch. It is a bug fix. But it matters because the bug could change the camera’s EI setting after you start recording if sensitivity was changed from the camera side display.

Why crews should care

ARRI says sensitivity is baked into Apple ProRes recordings. So if the camera jumps back to an older EI setting, your image can take a hit. You could end up underexposed, then fight noise later in the grade.

For ARRIRAW or ARRICORE, the issue is less damaging, but it can still mess with monitoring, metadata, and dailies. That is the kind of small problem that turns into a long post day.

The real lesson

Here is the simple takeaway: firmware is part of camera prep. Treat it like lens tests, media checks, and batteries.

If we are already on SUP 6.0.0, ARRI says we should update to 6.0.2. If we are on an older version and already in the middle of a shoot, ARRI does not recommend jumping to a new SUP during active production.

That is solid advice. Do not update a camera because the internet says there is a new file. Update because you tested it, your AC knows it, and the whole camera team is ready for it.

What we would do on set

Before a shoot, we would check the camera’s current SUP, read the release notes, run a short test, then record a few clips in the exact codec we plan to use.

We would also check exposure behavior from every control point: the side display, MVF-2, Camera Companion App, and Web Remote. If a bug is tied to one control path, that is where we want to catch it.

Bottom line

This update is small, but the lesson is big. The best camera is not just the one with the nicest image. It is the one that acts the way your crew expects when the pressure hits.
